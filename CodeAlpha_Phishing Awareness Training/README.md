What is Phishing? Phishing is a cyberattack technique where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, and banking details. Common phishing channels include: - Email phishing - SMS phishing (Smishing) - Voice phishing (Vishing) - Fake websites - Social media phishing

Email Phishing Example: "Your email account will be suspended within 24 hours. Click the link to verify now." Red Flags: - Urgent language - Unknown sender - Suspicious links - Generic greetings

Smishing (SMS Phishing) Example: "Your bank card is blocked. Click here to reactivate immediately." Warning Signs: - Shortened or suspicious links - Urgent action requests - Messages from unknown numbers

Vishing (Voice Phishing) Example: "This is your bank's fraud department. Please share your OTP to secure your account." Warning Signs: - Requests for OTPs or PINs - Pressure or fear tactics - Caller claiming authority

Fake Websites Example: Fake Microsoft login page using a look-alike domain (e.g., micros0ft-login[.]com ). Signs of Fake Websites: - Misspelled domain names - Missing HTTPS - Poor or copied design - Requests for unnecessary credentials

Social Media Phishing Example: "Congratulations! You are shortlisted for an interview. Click here for details." Common platforms used: - LinkedIn - Facebook - Instagram
